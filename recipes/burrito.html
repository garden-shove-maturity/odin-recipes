Burrito

Description

A burrito is a popular Mexican dish consisting of a large flour tortilla wrapped around a hearty filling

of rice, beans, meat, cheese, and various seasonings. Often enhanced with salsa, guacamole, or sour

cream, the burrito is rolled tightly into a portable bundle that’s both satisfying and flavorful. Its

versatility allows for endless variations—from classic beef and bean burritos to fresh vegetarian or

breakfast versions—making it a convenient and beloved meal around the world.

Ingredients

  • Flour tortillas
  • Cooked rice
  • Beans (black beans or pinto beans)
  • Seasoned meat (such as chicken, beef, or pork)
  • Cheese (often cheddar or Monterey Jack)

Steps

  1. Prepare the fillings - Cook and season the meat, rice, and beans; chop any vegetables or toppings.
  2. Warm the tortillas - Heat flour tortillas briefly on a pan or in the microwave so they’re soft and flexible.
  3. Assemble the burrito - Place rice, beans, meat, cheese, and other fillings in the center of the tortilla.
  4. Fold and roll - Fold the sides inward, then roll tightly from the bottom up to enclose the filling.
  5. Heat or serve - Toast the burrito on a pan for a crispy exterior, or serve it warm with salsa, guacamole, or sour cream.
