Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian baked dish made by layering wide,

flat pasta sheets with rich tomato sauce, creamy béchamel or ricotta,

and savory fillings such as seasoned ground meat, vegetables, and

cheese—typically mozzarella and Parmesan. Each layer melds together

in the oven, creating a comforting, hearty meal with a perfect balance

of textures: tender pasta, smooth sauce, and a golden, bubbling top.

Loved around the world, lasagna is both rustic and indulgent, often

served as the centerpiece of family gatherings or cozy dinners.

Ingredients

  • Lasagna noodles (pasta sheets)
  • Ground beef or Italian sausage
  • Tomato sauce (or marinara)
  • Ricotta or béchamel sauce
  • Mozzarella cheese

Steps

  1. Prepare the sauce - Cook ground beef or sausage with onions, garlic, and tomato sauce, then let it simmer to develop flavor.
  2. Boil the noodles - Cook lasagna sheets until just tender (or use no-boil noodles if preferred).
  3. Mix the cheese layer - Combine ricotta (or cottage cheese) with egg, herbs, and a bit of Parmesan.
  4. Assemble the layers - In a baking dish, alternate layers of sauce, noodles, cheese mixture, and mozzarella until filled.
  5. Bake - Cover with foil and bake until bubbling, then uncover to let the top brown and cheese melt beautifully.
